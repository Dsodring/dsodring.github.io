Updating My Portfolio

By Daniel Sødring on March 22, 2020

Link to my old portfolio

Changes To My Portfolio

A whole new colour scheme was chosen for the website, as the red and blue background felt a bit intense when reading long blog posts. The colour scheme now consists of an earthy background with headers in red, to create a slightly dramatic effect and this colour scheme also fits in well in scandiavian design and architecture.

The project page was also completely redesigned and now has a similar look to the blog page, with the ‘broken grid’ feel.

The contact page also had some subtle design changes, such as using sharp borders and a shadow on the send my message button to fit in with the rest of the site.

Changes Made Based On Feedback

During the portfolio showcase event, we had the opportunity to get valuable feedback from people working in the industry. As a result, I decided to make my portfolio more focused on web development, as that is the industry I wish to work in, and when talking to Adam Bee from Dept, a common thing he noticed in students is that they are trying to be the ‘jack of all trades’, ending up with little knowledge in many different fields, instead of developing a passion for one or two subjects and specialising in them. The landing page of my portfolio has been redesigned slightly to align more with my passion for front end development and the image has been made square to better fit in with the rest of the website.

Other feedback included swapping the menu and title in the nav, as traditionally the nav is on the right side and the logo is on the right.

The folder structure of my portfolio was also cleaned up and organised, the JavaScript files have been put in a separate folder and the images are sorted in different folders for easy access, the blog post HTML files was initially put in a folder by themselves in an effort to clean up the root folder, however, due to a limitation with GSAP, all HTML files are now placed on the root.

When changing the colour scheme, a number of different fonts were also tried out, Open Sans is still used as the font for body text, and both Montserrat and Bebas Neue was tested as header fonts, but based on the feedback gathered, it was decided to stay with Montserrat.

The page for individual projects have also been redesigned to have a similar look to the blog posts and more content has been added, such as images. The projects displayed on the project page have also been updated with newer projects.

Swup has also been replaced, and GSAP is now used to handle the animations for the content and page transitions, resulting in smoother and more customisable animations.
